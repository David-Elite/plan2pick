About the Destination

Kashmir is without a doubt India’s most beautiful and doomed land. The crown jewel that is Ladakh, situated at the rainy side of the Himalayas in the lap of Kashmir, is one of the most famous and most popular holidays for almost every Indian. At least once, in any season they prefer, it is a must visit for everyone. There is no time to visit Ladakh, as Ladakh has its own effect every season.

Rightly referred to as the Land of High Passes, Ladakh is home to some of the world’s highest roads (Umling La, Zoji La, and Khardung La, all over 18,000 ft), and has some of the most contrasting landscapes that make Ladakh even more beautiful and worthy of being more than just a destination. Ladakh is a dream, which is why it must be visited at least once by everyone.

FAQs

01

Which is the best time to visit Ladakh when all roads are open?

July and August are the definite peak months to visit Ladakh when all roads and passes are open. The weather is nippy with a sunny morning and afternoon making it fit for exploration. Expect the mercury to go up to 25 degree Celsius and fall to about 10 degree Celsius post sunset. For bikers too this is the best time to visit and experience all the wonderful colours the place is splashed in. Also the time when the popular Hemis Festival takes place.
